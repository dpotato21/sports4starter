Baseball

Basic Ideas

Baseball is another popular American sport. Similar to other sports, the main idea of Baseball is to be able to outscore the opponent. However, points are not socred through shooting or through touchdowns. They are scored through runs. A run in baseball is when a player hits the ball in between the foul lines and out of the field. However, that is not the only way to score. There are 4 bases, 1st base, 2nd base, 3rd base, and Home Plate. When a player is on third base, and the batter hits a fair ball, the player on base can run to homme plate and it would count as a run. Players on all bases can keep running to home base until they think they will be tagged out. 9 players from one team are allowed on the field at once. Those 9 players switch between offense and defense, similar to how basketball players must play both offense and defense. There are 9 innings in each game, which in Baseball terminology, is similar to a quarter in other sports. If the game is tied and more innings are required, then both teams receive extra innings under extra inning rules.

Terminology

  • Fair Hit: When a ball is hit and lands within the foul lines and is not caught by any fielders.
  • Base Hit: A hit that allows a batter to reach one of the bases.
  • Baseline: The white lines that connect 1st and 2nd base, and 2nd and 3rd base.
  • Batter: The player who is swinging and receiving pitches from the pitcher.
  • Pitcher: The player that is throwing the ball to the batter.
  • Batter's Box: The box which batters must stand at while batting.
  • Strike zone: Imaginary rectangle where the pitcher must pitch the ball in order for it be called a strike, even if the batter does not swing.
  • Single: When the batter hits the ball and can run to 1st base.
  • Double: When the batter hits the ball and can run to 2nd base.
  • Third: When the batter hits the ball and can run to 3rd base.
  • Home Run: When the batter hits the ball within the foul lines and out of the field.
  • Foul Ball: When the ball is hit outside of the foul lines.
  • Ball: When the ball is pitched outside of the strike zone.
  • Double Play: Recording 2 outs within one play.
  • Strike: When the ball is thrown within the strike zone without being hit, or when the batter swings for a ball and misses.
  • Dugout: The place where players are kept while the game is happening.
  • Grand Slam: A home run that occurs while there are players on all bases.
