Basketball

Basic Ideas

Basketball is one of the most popular sports in America. Want to finally find out what all they hype is about? So here's the deal. In basketball, the main objective is to score the ball in the hoop, and outscore your opponent. On the court, there are 10 players playing at all times. 5 from each team. However, they are not randomly assigned. They each have their own individual role as all 5 have different positions. The five positions are:

  • Point Guard
  • Shooting Guard
  • Small Forward
  • Power Forward
  • Center



They all in their own way contribute to score. There are three different ways to score points in basketball. If a player shoots the ball and makes it from behind the three point line, which is the farthest arc from the hoop, then they receive 3 points. If the player shoots the ball and makes it into the hoop from anywhere inside that arc, then it only counts as 2 points. If a player is fouled, depending from where they are on the court, they receive free throw shots. For example, if a player is fouled from outside the three point line, then they receive 3 free throw shots to make up for the 3 points that could have been scored. If they are fouled from inside the 3 point line where the shot would have been worth 2 points, then they are given 2 free throw shots. These free throw shots are taken from the free throw line, and every shot that is made counts as 1 point.

Terminology

  • Basketball: The ball used in play.
  • Hoop: The small circle that the ball must go through the receive points.
  • Dribble: The act of bouncing the balll on the floor to be able to move around the court.
  • Shot: Attempting to throw the ball into the hoop to receive points.
  • Air Ball: The act of shooting the ball but completely missing the rim and backboard.
  • Rim: The small usually red piece of steel surrounding the net.
  • Backboard: The large square that is behind the hoop. The hoop is connected to the backboard.
  • Rebound: The act of retrieving the ball when one team misses their shot.
  • Assist: When a player directly helps another play score points.
  • Double-Double: A player is considered to have a double-double when they record double digit numbers in 2 categories. For example, if a player gets 10 points and 10 rebounds, that player has a double-double.
  • Triple-Double: When a player is considered to have a triple-double when they record double digit numbers in 3 categories. For example, if a player gets 10 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists, that player has a triple-double.
  • Pass: Giving the ball to another player.
  • Fast Break: A fast offensive drive in an attempt to score before the defense is ready.
  • Charge: A foul that is called when an offensive player makes significant contact with a defensive player that is in a stationary position.
  • Paint: The small box area under the net
  • Shot clock: A device that tracks how much time a team has to score a point
  • Tip Off: The way the game begins to determine who gets possesion
